## One-line takeaway

They trained a linear time complexity RNN that can do long horizon tasks better than BPTT trained RNNs

## Problem

Traditional RNNs have to unroll their gradients throughout all the timesteps when doing backpropagation which causes vanishing gradients but also collapsing the entire state into one hidden state causes information retrieval problems.&#x20;

## Core idea

To use a transformer to effectively be a teacher for predicting good latent states during training, so that the RNN can better rollout during inference.&#x20;

This replaces the O(n) time complexity and makes it constant time. It's effectively supervised learning.&#x20;

## Method

They use a transformer encoder-decoder to predict in the same latent states as the RNNs would and make the RNN also predict the same latents. The decoder does normal predictions based on the encoder's latent outputs and uses the same cross entropy objective.&#x20;

The RNN during training has to predict the encoder's latents as closesly as possible, and we use MSE loss to compare them. This is effectively teacher forcing the RNN because it's also given the future input while asking to predict. The combined objective is:&#x20;

$$\mathcal{L}^{smt} \= \lambda\_{dec}\mathbb{E}\_t\left\[\mathcal{L}\_t^{dec}\right]&#x20;

\+ \lambda\_{dyn}\mathbb{E}\_t\left\[\mathcal{L}\_t^{dyn}\right]&#x20;

\+ \lambda\_{unif}\mathcal{L}^{unif}$$

During inference, the RNN would have to effectively do it's own inference.  &#x20;

## Results

**Setup:** evaluated RNN+BPTT, RNN+SMT, RNN+DMT mainly on tinystories, and a bunch of small synthetic tasks (retrieval, string copy, stack operations, key values, modular arithmetic).&#x20;

* Across the board, the RNN+SMT does well compared to the BPTT version. Their visuals weren't the most intuitive i have to admit.&#x20;
* They also performed scaling laws experiments and the RNN seems to scale well upto 10M parameters for these tasks.&#x20;

## Limitations

> While SMT is useful for learning how to encode sequences, it is not necessarily to be used for learning reasoning since intermediate steps are not supervised.

> The current SMT variant computes and trains only a single mt within a sequence. We found that training on all memories [m0, . . . , mT ] offered no improvement in our settings, but this may not hold at larger scales.

## Opinions and Questions

1. Would this scale to latent reasoning? Can RNNs do latent reasoning when given the right transformer latents?&#x20;
2. What would happen if the RNNs were given patches using a ViT encoder rather than just pixels or text?&#x20;
3. Tinystories is not exciting enough, and I think it's character level language modeling, would RNNs finally be able to do decently short context language modelling better than transformers if trained appropriately?&#x20;
4. What if we used a pretrained model and put an autoencoder to compress the latents and ask the RNN to replicate those?&#x20;
