* The CTM follows an internal time dimension called **ticks**, in other words the model spends t time steps recurrently processing the input.&#x20;
* Components include:&#x20;
  * Synapse net is a UNET style MLP that takes the concatenated outputs from the previous attention output and a z latent (not sure where this z latent comes from), they produce pre-activations.&#x20;
  * Neuron level models are MLPs that are considered neurons in this case that takes the outputs of the synapse network to produce the post activations.&#x20;
  * synchronization is defined as the inner product of histories of each neuron. Though in practicality it's not possible to do something like that so they opt for doing it for specific sets of neurons. They call this **neuron pairing.**&#x20;
  * To modulate the influence of past activity they also suggest using decay parameters.&#x20;
* The objective is defined as the combination of cross entropy loss + certainty (1-normalized entropy of the output distribution).&#x20;
* In the initial step, when there is no history the features are obtained from a backbone to get the pre activations. The backbone is used once for the given input and the CTM performs its internal ticks.&#x20;
* What makes this model interesting is its ability to "reason iteratively in latent space" where the confidence is not defined in one iteration but through a sequence of decisions taken iteratively, where at each iteration it might use different pieces of information to make the decision.&#x20;
* the model was tried in:&#x20;
  * Parity task
  * Maze with a resnet 34 backbone
  * cifar-10 with a resnet backbone&#x20;
  * sorting real numbers
* ablations were done for NLMs, synch, synapse net. Turns out having any of these not present really degrades the performance of these models.&#x20;
* Emergent behaviours TLDR:&#x20;
  * The dynamics are poor at init and the neurons dont exhibit any periodic nature.&#x20;
  * There might be dead neurons in there. (FUCK)
  * The CTM does a double take approach for solving the maze task, where in it solves it quickly and then does it slower.&#x20;
  * Sometimes it corrects itself while solving a task.&#x20;
  * Some of the attention heads were dedicated to a broader perspective of the maze (not sure what that means)
  * Under heavy constraints, the CTM would adopt for weird strategies that are interpretable but efficient (again, for the maze task).&#x20;
  * CTM exhibits adaptive computation. It can halt internal ticks based on **prediction certainty.**&#x20;
