## Problem

Recurrent models process sequences step by step, which blocks parallelization
and makes long-range dependencies hard to learn. Can we drop recurrence entirely?

## Key idea (one sentence)

Replace recurrence and convolution with **self-attention**, letting every
position attend to every other position directly.

## Method

Scaled dot-product attention is the core operation:

$$\text{Attention}(Q, K, V) = \text{softmax}\!\left(\frac{QK^\top}{\sqrt{d_k}}\right)V$$

Multiple attention "heads" run in parallel so the model can attend to different
representation subspaces. The inline scaling factor $\frac{1}{\sqrt{d_k}}$ keeps
the dot products from growing too large as $d_k$ increases.

## Results

State-of-the-art BLEU on WMT 2014 EN→DE and EN→FR, at a fraction of the training
cost of the best prior models.

## My take & questions

- The architecture is strikingly simple once recurrence is gone.
- How much of the gain is the attention mechanism vs. just better parallel
  scaling? Worth re-reading the ablations.
